The music industry generates $43 billion in revenues, but only 12% of that amount is the share of the artists generating content. Furthermore, artists have minimal control over how their music is distributed and little visibility into who is streaming it. To address these and other problems faced by artists, we are introducing JUU, a fully decentralized music streaming protocol built on the basis of a public blockchain infrastructure with the application of various decentralized technologies

Music Marketplace

Digital technology is revolutionizing the availability and consumption of music, but it is also posing serious problems for distribution. Accessing, downloading and using music for free through YouTube and other platforms is easy.

For subscription-based platforms, such as Spotify and Apple Music, the way artists are compensated for the music they create has not changed significantly — meaning that even after service providers and labels split their shares, some artists are still paid little. Furthermore, anyone can alter the music available on said platforms with any decent software package, mislabel it, and upload it under a different account.

Over the past few years, blockchain has become widespread across all industries. Thanks to blockchain technologies and tokenized artworks, independent music labels and artists are reaping the benefits and regaining control of their money and artwork.

All eyes are on blockchain. The decentralization of the music industry can eliminate the elitism of music production and distribution and put money back in the pockets of artists — its real owners. NFTs are a development that could help revolutionize how indie artists are compensated and recognized.

blockchain ticket sales

Preventing fake ticket sales and scams. Blockchain provides a single source of truth for both ticket holders and organizers. The transfer of NFTs from the initial sale to resale is stored on the blockchain immutably, so that all parties can prove the ticket’s authenticity. In cases where the resale of tickets is forbidden, the NFTs can be programmed to be non-transferrable, not to be moved to another buyer physically.

Reduction of costs. The costs associated with selling and minting NFTs are negligible compared to the traditional ticketing system. You can get an unforgeable ticket at a lower production cost so customers and organizers can validate the authenticity of every ticket on-chain and track the history of ownership.

Quick production. An NFT can be minted and be ready to sell in less than a minute.

Perpetual revenue. Since programmable NFTs can have built-in rules for merchandise, content, resales and royalty splits, the organizer can analyze profit-sharing percentages for future resales or creative content on secondary markets and receive funds, knowing that they are unalterable within the NFT’s coding.

New revenue opportunities. NFT-based tickets act as programmable money, providing unlimited potential for new revenue opportunities.
